WHY IT WORKS ?
Crowdfunding - The Concept

Crowdfunding is the (popular) practice of funding a project or venture by raising monetary contributions from a large number of people, typically via the internet.

The crowdfunding model is fueled by three types of actors: the project initiator (student) who proposes the idea and/or project to be funded; individuals or groups (the “backers”, you) who support the idea; and a moderating organization (the "platform", Lakshya) that brings the parties together to launch the idea.

How this works

A student uploads details of the project his or her team wants to undertake, along with other information such as the team’s past work, timelines, prototypes/simulations created etc.

The team also provide the total funding goal and campaign period. For example, a team of students wants to raise Rs.50,000 (funding goal) in 30 days (campaign period).

After a basic vetting process, ideas are uploaded to the platform for everyone to see.

You (the backer) can go through all the project ideas displayed here and, if an idea catches your fancy, you can pledge any amount. If the team reaches its funding goal in the campaign period, you will be sent an email to fulfill your pledge via the website (using your card or net banking).

Democratize Access to Funding

Lakshya's crowd funding platform aims to give every student the chance to shape ideas into reality. Given the right tools, guidance and exposure, students can come up with some of the most amazing ideas. However, asking them to go through an application process every year severely limits our reach, as we’re able to fund only a handful of project ideas. This platform gives everyone an equal chance to raise funding.

How will funding be used

While uploading their ideas, students will also provide a brief of how they intend to use the funding. This can be used to purchase equipment, material, labour, and other products or services. It might also be used to cover their travel and other project related expenses
